If there’s one thing that we can all agree on, it’s that us scientists (and programmers) have an infinite appetite for learning.

So we thought we’d share with you some of the most interesting things we’ve recently read. Enjoy!

Optimizing Journalism for Trust
NYU journalism professor Jay Rosen outlines his thoughts on how to re-build trust in news media, as part of being advisor to Dutch news website De Correspondent. ⇉

“The problem is not how to generate trust by publishing news. Trump does that with his Twitter feed, in a way.”
How to Write a Science Blog Post that People Want to Read
Gem-filled presentation by science communicator and educator Michelle Kovacevic, who hands out practical tips to improve any science writer's craft, regardless of experience. ⇉

“Academics use the passive voice a lot because they want readers to focus on the result of an action rather than the person doing the action. The passive voice makes storytelling more difficult because it hides the characters deep in the sentence — if it shows them at all.”
The Scientific Paper is Obsolete
James Somers, programmer and contributing editor at The Atlantic, puts forward his case for how absurd it is that any technical publications involving “any sort of data or mathematics or modeling or graphs or plots” should still remain in paper format. ⇉

“[Theodore Gray, former researcher at Wolfram Research commented,] ‘It’s incalculable, literally…how much is lost, and how much time is wasted.’ ”
